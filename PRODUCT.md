# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

MTG players in a specific Discord community — invite-only or semi-private access. They arrive task-driven: they know what they want, they have a decklist, and they're here to turn it into an order quickly. The primary user is a player who needs singles for a deck and prefers bulk proxy orders over hunting individual cards on open marketplaces. They trust the community, not the brand.

## Product Purpose

A private group-buy storefront for bootleg MTG proxies. Members browse a card catalog, build a cart, or import a decklist (Moxfield/Archidekt) and check out in one shot. The bulk purchasing model cuts per-card costs versus individual marketplace orders. Success is a frictionless path from "I need these cards" to "order placed."

## Positioning

The single place our community buys bootleg proxies together — faster, cheaper, and less hassle than anywhere else.

## Brand Personality

Underground but polished. This is a proxy shop built for a Discord server, not a startup. It should feel premium despite what it sells: confident, direct, and clearly made by people who play the game. Voice is informal and community-native; visuals are tight and intentional, not generic storefront chrome.

## Anti-references

- Generic TCGO-style storefronts (TCGPlayer, Card Kingdom clone aesthetic — the "official card store" look)
- Cheap proxy sites — watermarks, awkward layouts, discount-bin energy
- Overdesigned SaaS dashboards — corporate, cold, feature-announcement UI

## Design Principles

1. **Speed over ceremony.** Every workflow (browse → cart → checkout, import → order) should feel fast and direct. Remove friction, not steps.
2. **Underground credibility.** The product is bootleg proxies. Don't sanitize that. Lean into the irreverence — polished execution makes it credible, not corporate branding.
3. **Clarity at density.** Card catalogs are information-dense by nature. Hierarchy and scan-ability matter more than whitespace and elegance.
4. **Community-native, not public-facing.** Design decisions should feel like they were made by someone who plays the game and knows the people in the server — not optimized for acquisition or trust-building with strangers.
5. **Dark by default, deliberately.** This is a late-night hobby purchase, not a productivity tool. The dark mode isn't a theme toggle; it's the default register.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target, but strong readability is non-negotiable — especially for card names, pricing, and stock status at density. No specific accommodation requirements beyond that baseline.
